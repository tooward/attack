## Getting Started

### What is Five Rings?

Five Rings is a mobile fighting game inspired by classic 2D fighters like Street Fighter. Fight against AI opponents or other players using precise timing, strategic spacing, and powerful special moves.

### First Launch

1. **Character Select** - Choose your fighter (currently: Musashi)
2. **Game Mode** - Select Practice Mode, Single Player, or Online Multiplayer
3. **Fight!** - Learn the basics and master your character

### Game Objectives

- Reduce opponent's health to zero before time runs out
- Win 2 rounds to win the match
- Use normal attacks, special moves, and combos to deal damage
- Block and evade to avoid damage

## Combat System

### Basic Attacks

**Light Attacks (LP, LK):**
- Fast startup (5-6 frames)
- Low damage (8-10)
- Safe on block
- Good for starting combos

**Heavy Attacks (HP, HK):**
- Slow startup (12-15 frames)
- High damage (15-20)
- Punishable on block
- Good for punishing mistakes

**Crouching Attacks:**
- Press ‚Üì + button
- Hit low (must block low)
- Some can start combos
- Generally safer than standing

**Jumping Attacks:**
- Press button in air
- Hit overhead (must block standing)
- Can cross up (land behind opponent)
- Risky if blocked

### Throws

**Command:** Close range + THROW button  
**Damage:** 12  
**Startup:** 5 frames  
**Range:** 1.5 units

**Properties:**
- Unblockable
- Can be teched (press THROW when grabbed)
- Leaves opponent at neutral distance
- Cannot throw airborne opponents

**When to Use:**
- Against defensive opponents who block too much
- After blocked moves with advantage
- In mixup situations

### Blocking

**Standing Block:** Hold ‚Üê (hold away from opponent)  
**Crouching Block:** Hold ‚Üô (down-back)

**What Blocks:**
- Standing block: Highs and mids
- Crouching block: Lows and mids
- Neither blocks throws

**Block Stun:**
- Light attacks: +2 to -2 frames
- Heavy attacks: -4 to -8 frames
- Special moves: Varies

**Chip Damage:**
- No chip damage on normals
- Special moves deal 10% chip damage when blocked
- Cannot be KO'd by chip (leaves 1 HP)

### Frame Advantage

**Advantage = Your recovery - Opponent's blockstun**

**Positive (+):** You recover first, can attack  
**Neutral (0):** Both recover same time  
**Negative (-):** Opponent recovers first, you're vulnerable

**Example:**
- Your move is -2 on block
- Opponent's fastest move is 5 frames
- They can punish with any move 7 frames or faster

### Combos

**Cancel System:**
- Light ‚Üí Heavy (always cancellable)
- Normal ‚Üí Special (cancellable on hit)
- Special ‚Üí Super (requires meter, coming soon)

**Damage Scaling:**
- First hit: 100%
- Second hit: 80%
- Third hit: 60%
- Fourth+ hits: 50%

**Example Combos:**

**Musashi:**
1. LP ‚Üí HP ‚Üí Hadoken (Light) = 28 damage
2. Jumping HK ‚Üí Crouching LP ‚Üí HP ‚Üí Shoryuken (Heavy) = 42 damage
3. Crouching LK ‚Üí Standing HP ‚Üí Hurricane Kick (Heavy) = 36 damage

## Strategy Guide

### Universal Strategy

**Spacing:**
- Stay at your character's optimal range
- Musashi: Mid-range (3-4 units)
- Kaze: Close range (1-2 units)
- Tetsuo: Throw range (1.5-2 units)

**Footsies:**
- Use pokes (standing LK, crouching LP) to control space
- Whiff punish: Make opponent miss, then attack
- Don't overcommit to heavy attacks

**Defense:**
- Block high by default
- React to low attacks
- Tech throws (press THROW when grabbed)
- Use invincible moves on wake-up (risky)

**Offense:**
- Mix up throws and attacks
- Use frame advantage to pressure
- Don't be predictable
- Know your combo conversions

### Character-Specific Tips

**Playing as Musashi:**
1. Control space with Hadoken
2. Anti-air jumps with Shoryuken
3. Use walk speed to maintain range
4. Punish whiffed attacks with HP ‚Üí Hadoken
5. Don't spam projectiles - opponent can jump in

**Fighting Against Musashi:**
- As Kaze: Use Air Dash to get over projectiles
- As Tetsuo: Walk and block, use armor moves
- Universal: Jump at mid-range, not full screen

## FAQ

**Q: How do I perform special moves?**  
A: Input the motion (like ‚Üì‚Üò‚Üí) then press the button. You have 10 frames (167ms) to complete the input. Watch for the motion trail on the D-pad.

**Q: Why can't I do a combo?**  
A: Make sure you're cancelling properly. Light attacks can cancel into heavy attacks, and normals can cancel into specials on hit. Practice the timing in Practice Mode.

**Q: How do I beat the AI?**  
A: Start with Tutorial bot at difficulty 1. Focus on blocking and anti-airing. As you improve, increase the difficulty. Different bot types require different strategies.

**Q: What's the best character?**  
A: All characters are designed to be balanced. Musashi is beginner-friendly, Kaze rewards aggressive play, and Tetsuo rewards patience and reads.

**Q: Can I change controls?**  
A: Yes! Go to Settings ‚Üí Controls to adjust button layout, size, and sensitivity.

**Q: When is online multiplayer coming?**  
A: Online multiplayer is planned for a future update. Follow development on GitHub for updates.

**Q: How do I unlock more characters?**  
A: Currently, Kaze and Tetsuo are in development. They'll be available in the next major update (v1.0).
